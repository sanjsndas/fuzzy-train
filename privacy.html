Privacy Policy

Our commitment to protecting your personal information and ensuring your digital safety

At Greenhatchery Care, we take your privacy seriously. This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services. Please read this policy carefully.

1. Information Collection Methods

We collect information when you register on our site, place an order, subscribe to our newsletter, respond to a survey, or fill out a form. This includes both personal identification information (name, email, phone) and non-personal identification information (browser type, technical details). Information is gathered through various methods including direct input, cookies, and analytics tools to enhance user experience.

2. Types of Data Collected

The types of personal data we collect include contact information, demographic information, payment details, and preferences. We also automatically collect technical data about equipment, browsing actions, and patterns. This comprehensive data collection helps us personalize services while respecting your privacy rights and preferences.

3. Purpose of Data Collection

We collect your information to provide and improve our services, process transactions, send periodic emails, improve customer service, and personalize user experience. The data helps us understand customer needs better and deliver more relevant content, offers, and services tailored to individual preferences.

4. Data Protection Measures

We implement a variety of security measures including encryption, firewalls, access controls, and regular security audits to safeguard your personal information. All sensitive payment information is transmitted via Secure Socket Layer (SSL) technology and stored with AES-256 encryption to ensure maximum protection.

5. Cookie Usage Policy

Our website uses cookies to enhance user experience, analyze site traffic, and personalize content. Cookies are small files placed on your device that allow us to recognize returning visitors. You can choose to disable cookies through your browser settings, though this may affect certain site functionalities.

6. Third-party Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or transfer your personally identifiable information to outside parties without providing users advance notice. This excludes trusted third parties who assist in website operation or servicing you, as long as these parties agree to keep information confidential.

7. Third-party Links

Occasionally, we may include third-party products or services on our website. These third-party sites have separate privacy policies, and we bear no responsibility for their content or activities. We encourage users to review the privacy policies of any external sites they visit.

8. GDPR Compliance

We comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for users in the European Union. This includes providing clear information about data collection, implementing privacy by design, honoring user rights to access/delete data, and appointing a Data Protection Officer to oversee compliance.

9. CCPA Compliance

For California residents, we comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) by providing clear notices about data collection and respecting user rights to know, delete, and opt-out of the sale of personal information. California consumers can make privacy rights requests through our designated portal.

10. User Rights & Choices

Users have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of personal data. You may opt-out of marketing communications at any time by clicking unsubscribe links or contacting us directly. We provide mechanisms for users to update preferences or request account deletion in accordance with applicable laws.

11. Data Retention Periods

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes collected or as required by law. Transaction data is typically retained for 7 years for tax purposes, while inactive account data may be deleted after 2 years. Automated systems regularly purge obsolete records.

12. Children's Online Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children without parental consent. If we discover that a child under 13 has provided personal information without verification, we immediately delete such information.

13. International Data Transfers

As an international company, we may transfer and process data outside your country. We implement appropriate safeguards like standard contractual clauses when transferring EEA personal data to third countries to ensure adequate protection levels regardless where data is processed.

14. Marketing Communications

With your consent, we may send promotional emails about new products, special offers, or other information we think you may find interesting. These communications include unsubscribe options, and we honor opt-out requests promptly, typically within 10 business days.

15. Data Breach Procedures

In the event of a data breach, we have established response plans including internal reporting systems, assessment protocols, notification procedures (following regulatory timelines), and remediation measures. Users affected by a breach will be notified promptly with guidance on protective steps.

16. Automated Decision Making

We may use automated processing, including profiling, to analyze personal aspects of users for marketing purposes. These automated processes help personalize content but never result in legally significant automated decisions. Users may request human intervention or contest automated decisions.

17. Sensitive Data Handling

We implement enhanced protections for sensitive personal data including health information or biometric data. Such information requires explicit opt-in consent, is processed separately, and receives additional security measures like pseudonymization where appropriate.

18. Payment Processing Security

All payment transactions undergo PCI-DSS compliant processing through certified providers. We never store complete payment card details - only tokenized references and the last four digits are retained for identification purposes with payment processors handling the sensitive data securely.

19. Website Analytics Usage

We use analytics tools to understand user behavior, improve site navigation, and enhance content relevance. Analytical data is aggregated and anonymized where possible. Users can opt-out of analytics tracking through cookie preferences or browser extensions.

20. Data Minimization Principles

We adhere to data minimization principles by collecting only what is necessary for specified purposes. Unnecessary data fields are eliminated from forms, and periodic reviews ensure we maintain only current, relevant information aligned with business needs.

21. Employee Access Controls

Employee access to personal data follows strict need-to-know principles with role-based permissions. Training programs ensure all staff understand privacy obligations. Access is logged and regularly audited to prevent unauthorized use or accidental disclosures.

22. Service Provider Oversight

Third-party service providers handling personal data must meet stringent contractual privacy obligations matching our standards. We conduct due diligence before engagement and periodic audits to verify continued compliance with data protection requirements.

23. Privacy by Design Implementation

We embed privacy considerations into all stages of product development through Privacy by Design principles. New projects undergo Privacy Impact Assessments, and systems default to the most privacy-friendly settings without compromising functionality.

24. Data Subject Request Handling

We maintain efficient processes for handling data subject requests including access, rectification, erasure, and portability requirements. Requests are typically fulfilled within 30 days, with identity verification required to prevent unauthorized disclosures.

25. Record Keeping Requirements

We maintain detailed records of processing activities as required by privacy regulations. These records document data categories, processing purposes, retention periods, and security measures to demonstrate compliance during regulatory inquiries.

26. Consent Management

Where consent is required for processing, we implement granular opt-in mechanisms with clear affirmative actions. Consent is documented with timestamps, and withdrawal mechanisms are as simple as giving consent with no detriment for withdrawal.

27. Data Accuracy Maintenance

We implement measures to ensure personal data remains accurate and up-to-date. Users are encouraged to update their information, and automated validation helps identify outdated records needing review through periodic reconciliation processes.

28. Legitimate Interest Assessments

When processing data under legitimate interest grounds, we conduct documented balancing tests evaluating necessity against individual rights and freedoms. These assessments are reviewed annually or when processing purposes change significantly.

29. Pseudonymization Techniques

Where appropriate, we employ pseudonymization techniques to enhance privacy protection. This involves processing data in ways that can no longer be attributed to specific individuals without additional information kept separately and securely

30. Data Breach Notification Procedures

In the event of a data breach, we have established protocols to notify affected individuals and relevant authorities within the required timeframes. Notifications include details about the nature of the breach, potential consequences, and recommended actions for affected individuals.

31. Privacy Training for Employees

All employees undergo regular privacy training to ensure they understand their responsibilities regarding data protection. Training covers topics such as data handling best practices, recognizing phishing attempts, and the importance of safeguarding personal information.

32. Privacy Policy Updates

We may update this privacy policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Users will be notified of significant changes via email or through a notice on our website. We encourage users to review this policy regularly for the latest information on our privacy practices.

33. User Feedback and Complaints

We value user feedback regarding our privacy practices. Users can submit complaints or concerns about our data handling through our contact form. We take all complaints seriously and will investigate and respond promptly to resolve any issues.

34. Data Protection Officer Contact

We have appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) responsible for overseeing compliance with data protection laws. Users can contact the DPO directly for inquiries related to personal data processing, privacy rights, or to report concerns.

35. Privacy Impact Assessments

We conduct Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) for new projects or significant changes to existing processes that may impact user privacy. PIAs help identify potential risks and ensure that privacy considerations are integrated into project planning and execution.

36. Data Sharing with Affiliates

We may share personal data with our affiliates for operational purposes, such as improving services or conducting joint marketing efforts. All affiliates are required to adhere to our privacy standards and protect user data in accordance with this policy.

37. User Consent for Marketing

We obtain explicit consent from users before sending marketing communications. Users can opt-in to receive promotional content and can easily opt-out at any time through unsubscribe links provided in our emails.

38. Data Portability Rights

Users have the right to request a copy of their personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. We facilitate data portability requests to allow users to transfer their data to other service providers if desired.

39. Privacy Notices for Specific Services

For certain services, we may provide additional privacy notices that explain how we handle personal data specific to those services. These notices will be provided at the point of data collection and will supplement this privacy policy.
